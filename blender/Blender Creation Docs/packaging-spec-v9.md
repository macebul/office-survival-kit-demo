## 1. Concept: The Stolen Kit

Two characters drive the entire packaging experience.

**The OG** is the original creator — a dry, deadpan, underground voice. Someone who survived the corporate machine and built the first Office Survival Kit as a genuine contraband manual. Handmade, passed around, never meant to be a product. The OG's voice is wry, knowing, and speaks directly to the recipient as a peer. Think: a note left in a desk drawer by someone who quit before you started.

**The Corporate Nemesis** is a sleazy marketing executive who discovered the OG's kit, stole the concept, mass-produced it, and now sells it on Amazon as a retail product. The nemesis has repackaged every item in the kit into individual retail-style tuck-end boxes, slapped corporate branding on everything, and wrapped it all in a polished Amazon shipping box. The nemesis is the voice of every soulless product launch, every "synergy" meeting, every forced fun initiative. The nemesis's aesthetic is clean, corporate, and deeply punchable.

**The tension:** The retail packaging IS the nemesis's product. The briefcase, the outer box, the individual item boxes — all of it reads as the nemesis's mass-produced version of something that was once handmade and personal. But the OG has fought back. Throughout the packaging, OG sticky notes appear — printed graphics designed to look like handwritten sticky notes stuck onto the nemesis's corporate packaging. Inside the hidden compartment, the OG's Activity Guide is smuggled in. The nemesis controls the surface; the OG hides in the margins.

The recipient experiences both voices: the nemesis's slick retail presentation and the OG's subversive commentary. The humor comes from the contrast — corporate polish undermined by underground truth.

### Chalk Outline & Die-Cut Recess Style

Every item box in every tray sits within a die-cut recess (Pelican case style) — a molded cutout shaped precisely to each item's RETAIL BOX (not the raw item). This is not an open tray with loose boxes; each box has a fitted pocket that prevents shifting and provides a premium unboxing feel.

Each die-cut recess has a chalk-outline silhouette printed on the tray surface surrounding the cutout, plus the item name and a short tagline printed adjacent to the recess. The chalk outlines serve three purposes: they tell the recipient where each item goes (and that one is "missing" if removed), they reinforce the crime-scene / institutional world, and they provide a clean, premium presentation that reads as intentional rather than cluttered.

**Exception: Confiscated Stapler Recess.** One recess in Tray 2 is intentionally EMPTY — a small stapler silhouette with a **prison orange chalk outline** (not white/cream like the other chalk outlines — it stands out). An OG sticky note is printed on the tray surface near the recess with commentary. The label reads: "CONFISCATED — WEAPONIZED OFFICE EQUIPMENT. INCIDENT #147. DO NOT REISSUE." The absence of the item is the punchline. See Section 7 for details.

Outlines are printed in white or cream on the darker tray floor. Line weight should feel hand-drawn — not perfectly uniform, not digitally precise. Slightly rough edges. Think actual chalk on a dark surface, not vector art. Recess shapes match the ITEM BOXES, not the raw items inside.

## 5. Interior Structure: Two-Zone Layout

### Zone Overview

The interior is divided into two zones by a full-height divider wall:

**Front Zone (facing the person):** Trays 1 and 2 stacked vertically with document sets between them. Trays are full interior width (no ring clearance needed) and centered in the front zone. Each tray contains individually boxed items in Pelican-style die-cut recesses sized for the boxes.

**Back Zone (along back wall):** A hidden padded compartment containing the Mug Shot mug (on its side, handle to the side) nestled in shredded "CLASSIFIED" paper padding. Completely hidden while trays are in place. Revealed when Tray 2 is lifted and a J-shaped compartment cover is removed. The Activity Guide sits on the briefcase floor in the front zone (under the trays), not in the mug compartment — it is discovered as a final easter egg when both trays are lifted out.

*For all zone dimensions and clearance values, see Physical Dimensions & Sourcing Spec. Key constraint: back zone is the tightest fit (~0.44" clearance).*

## 7. Tray Specifications

### Tray Design Principles (Both Trays)

- **Die-cut recesses (Pelican case style) SIZED FOR BOXES:** Each item's retail box sits in a precisely shaped molded cutout — not an open tray with loose items. Recesses are shaped to fit the PRODUCT BOXES, not the raw items inside. Each recess has a chalk-outline silhouette printed on the tray surface surrounding the cutout, plus the item name and a short tagline printed adjacent to the recess. This provides a premium unboxing feel and prevents all shifting during shipping.
- Copy on tray surfaces includes item names and short taglines next to each die-cut recess — the extra inch of case length (14" vs. 13") provides space for this copy alongside the cutouts.
- Story lives in the document sets; trays are the payoff, not the explanation. Tray copy is labels and taglines, not paragraphs.
- Each tray lifts out independently via hybrid lift tabs (see Section 9).
- Tray material: rigid paperboard or molded pulp. Dark carbon fiber texture finish — very dark charcoal/near-black with subtle sheen. Prison orange accent trim on top edge border and inside finger notches.
- Trays are full interior width of the front zone — no ring clearance needed.
- **Tray undersides are easter egg surfaces** — visible when each tray is lifted out (see Section 13).

### Shipping Considerations

- Mug lying on its side in shredded paper padding — must prevent all movement during transit. Confirm adequate protection with drop test; thin foam sheet at bottom as backup if needed.
- Outer Amazon box provides an additional layer of shipping protection around the briefcase.
- Divider wall adds structural rigidity but also complexity — factor into assembly cost.
- J-shaped compartment cover must not shift during shipping (floor flap is held down by tray weight above; top surface rests on divider wall and back wall).
- Belly band provides additional closure security during transit.
- Total assembled weight TBD after all items are sourced.

- Back zone clearance is ~0.44" — tight but workable; confirm with prototype.
- Individual item boxes in die-cut recesses should not rattle — recesses must be snug fits.

## 13. Hidden Surfaces & Easter Eggs

### Easter Egg Philosophy

Hidden surfaces are bonus moments. They reward the curious, the thorough, the person who flips things over and looks underneath. Not everyone will find them — that's the point. When someone discovers one, it should feel personal, like the OG left it just for them.

### Easter Egg Inventory

| Surface | When Discovered | Copy Direction |
|---------|-----------------|----------------|
| Tray 1 underside | When Tray 1 is lifted out (Step 10) | OG character message — first hidden breadcrumb. Visible to anyone who lifts the tray. |
| Tray 2 underside | When Tray 2 is lifted out (Step 14) | OG character message — second hidden breadcrumb. May reference the first. |
| Tray wall interiors (both trays) | When item boxes are removed from recesses | Short OG messages — only found by people who empty trays completely and look at the inside walls. |
| Item box interiors (optional) | When boxes are opened and unfolded | OG message printed inside box flap — only found by people who inspect the box itself, not just the item. |

| Inside the mug | When someone looks inside the mug (Step 17) | Final easter egg — small card. The LAST discovery in the entire kit. |
| Base floor (front zone) | When everything is removed | Final message from OG. Only found by complete unpackers. |
| Base floor (back zone) | When mug and paper are removed | Additional final message. Only found by complete unpackers. |

### OG Sticky Notes as Easter Eggs

The OG sticky note graphics serve double duty: some are visible immediately (on box fronts, on tray surfaces), while others are hidden (on box backs, inside flaps, in less obvious positions). The visible ones establish the pattern; the hidden ones reward closer inspection. The recipient learns: look for the sticky notes. The OG is hiding everywhere.
