# Office Survival Kit — Exterior Copy

**Last Updated:** March 16, 2026
**Status:** Draft v4 — Label Enhancement Pass. Humor punch-up, absurdist push, character expansion.
**Purpose:** All exterior surface copy in one place, organized by Illustrator artboard order.
**Diff from v3:** Every change annotated with `▸ CHANGE:` notes. Elements with no changes marked `▸ NO CHANGE`.

> **Reference:** For visual treatments, surface positions, and material specifications of each element, see the Exterior Spec.

> **Voice Layer Note (NEW):** The briefcase exterior is the OG's original institutional kit — but the nemesis has been here. Corporate-flavored labels (IT Asset Tag, Inspected By) are his additions. Where noted, an OG sticky note annotation reacts to the nemesis's touch. The exterior is where the two voices collide.

## ARTBOARD 2 — Front Face (14.0" × 5.0")

*Handle and clasps are also on this surface but do not carry copy. See Exterior Spec for their specifications.*

### Element 5: IT Asset Tag
**Treatment:** Small printed label, clinical institutional format — like the stickers found on office laptops. This is a NEMESIS addition (corporate overlay on the OG's kit).

> ASSET TAG: #000001
> PROPERTY OF: NOBODY ANYMORE
> PREV. ASSIGNED: [REDACTED BY HR]
> DEPT: BETWEEN DEPARTMENTS
> STATUS: UNSUPERVISED
> CONDITION: HOLDING IT TOGETHER
> DO NOT REMOVE UNDER PENALTY OF MONDAY

▸ CHANGE: Added 3 new fields that escalate the absurdity. "PREV. ASSIGNED: [REDACTED BY HR]" implies someone had this kit and something happened to them. "BETWEEN DEPARTMENTS" is funnier than "N/A" — it's corporate limbo. "UNSUPERVISED" is funnier than "DEPLOYED" — implies the kit is loose. "HOLDING IT TOGETHER" is doing double duty (physical condition + emotional state). Removed "DEPT: N/A" (replaced with "BETWEEN DEPARTMENTS" line). Original "PROPERTY OF: NOBODY ANYMORE" and "PENALTY OF MONDAY" kept — both land.

▸ NEW — OG STICKY NOTE: Small handwritten-style annotation near this label:
> *"He put an asset tag on it. An ASSET TAG. Like it's a laptop."*

This is the first visible collision of the two voices on the exterior. The OG is reacting to the nemesis's corporate labeling with disbelief. Positions the OG for anyone reading the outside before opening. Treatment: small cream/yellow torn-edge note graphic, handwritten font, slightly angled. Same visual language as interior OG notes.

### Element 6: INSPECTED BY
**Treatment:** Pre-printed QC sticker — white rectangle, clean clinical font, authentic peel-and-stick look. NOT a rubber stamp. This is a NEMESIS addition (QC theater).

> INSPECTED BY: GARY
> STATION: 14
> DATE: [blank]
> SHIFT: 3RD MONDAY IN A ROW
> APPROVED ✓
> NOTES: Everything checks out. I don't want to talk about the stapler.

▸ CHANGE: Expanded significantly. Added STATION (institutional detail), SHIFT line (the joke: Gary has been inspecting things on Mondays — the worst day — for three weeks straight, and he's tracking it). The NOTES field is the real payoff — Gary's voice comes through for the first time. "I don't want to talk about the stapler" plants a seed for the confiscated stapler recess inside. Gary is now a minor character with a personality: dutiful, slightly haunted, compliant but aware. This is Gary's 1st exterior appearance (he may recur inside). Kept "DATE: [blank]" — the empty field is funnier than any date.

▸ CHARACTER NOTE: Gary is the #2 recurring character (2-3 total mentions across kit). He's the quality inspector who has seen things. He is not Kevin.

### Element 9: Ingredients Tag
**Treatment:** Smaller printed label, positioned below or adjacent to Nutrition Facts

> **INGREDIENTS:** Unread email threads (2,847), artificial urgency, one coworker who pronounces it "supposably," recycled air (vintage 2004), the persistent feeling that someone is disappointed in you, fluorescent lighting rated for interrogation use, a printer that senses fear, Kevin.
>
> Contains: Traces of ambition (processed in a facility that once supported dreams. Discontinued 2019).
>
> **ALLERGEN WARNING:** May contain Diane from HR.

▸ CHANGES:
1. **"Unread email threads" → "Unread email threads (2,847)"** — the specific number is funnier. Makes it feel measured.
2. **"one coworker who schedules 8am meetings" → "one coworker who pronounces it 'supposably'"** — more visceral, more specific, more universally rage-inducing. The original "8am meetings" is now in the Nutrition Facts territory.
3. **"recycled air" → "recycled air (vintage 2004)"** — "vintage" applied to recirculated office air is absurd. The specific year implies it hasn't been refreshed in 22 years.
4. **"the vague sense" → "the persistent feeling"** — "persistent" is more clinical and slightly more unhinged than "vague."
5. **"printer that only jams when you're already late" → "a printer that senses fear"** — Shorter, weirder, funnier. Implies the printer is sentient and hostile.
6. **"may have been processed in a facility that no longer supports dreams" → "processed in a facility that once supported dreams. Discontinued 2019."** — "Discontinued 2019" is more specific and reads like a product recall. The period before it creates a pause.
7. **NEW: "ALLERGEN WARNING: May contain Diane from HR."** — Introduces Diane as character #3 (1-2 total mentions). The allergen warning format is perfect for HR — it frames Diane as a substance you might have an adverse reaction to. Her full name+department is given clinically, exactly how an allergen would be listed. This is Diane's 1st appearance.
