# Office Survival Kit — Binder-to-Booklet Conversion Guide

**Created:** March 10, 2026
**Purpose:** Complete instructions for converting the interior system from a single D-ring binder with loose page sections to three individual coil-bound booklets. Organized by document so work can be done in parallel across multiple chat windows.

---

## THE CHANGE

**Old system:** Three D-ring binders mounted to the interior back wall (-Y). 9-12 loose pages threaded through rings in three sections, alternating with trays. Pages flip over rings and rest on open lid.

**New system:** Three individual coil-bound booklets. Each booklet sits on top of its corresponding tray, completely covering the tray contents below. No ring hardware. No mounting to the case walls. Each booklet is a self-contained document the recipient picks up, reads, and sets aside to reveal the tray beneath.

### Booklet Dimensions (CRITICAL — must be resolved before other changes)

**⚠️ TWO OPEN QUESTIONS MUST BE ANSWERED FIRST:**

**Question 1: Booklet footprint.** Each booklet sits on top of a tray and must cover the tray contents enough that items below aren't immediately visible. The tray interior footprint is now **12.5" × 7.5"** (wider than before — see tray width change below). The booklets don't need to match this exactly, but they need to be large enough to create a "cover" effect. Options:
- **Full coverage:** Booklet is ~12" × 7" (nearly fills tray). Large format — impressive, but unusual for coil binding at this size. Check sourcing feasibility.
- **Partial coverage:** Booklet is ~8.5" × 7" or ~9" × 6" (covers most of tray, some items may peek around edges). More standard booklet size, easier to source.
- **Small format:** Booklet is ~5.5" × 4.25" or similar (half-letter size). Sits centered on tray, doesn't cover items. Items are visible around the booklet. Less dramatic reveal, but cheapest and easiest to produce. Still reads as "document sitting on top of your gear."

**Recommendation:** Partial coverage (~8.5" × 7" or similar) balances reveal impact with production feasibility. But this needs sourcing input.

**Question 2: Booklet thickness and stack math.** Each booklet adds height to the stack. The current stack fills exactly 6.5" with essentially zero thickness for pages. Coil-bound booklets are thicker:

| Page Count | Estimated Closed Thickness (with coil) |
|---|---|
| 4–6 pages (8–12 sheets) | ~0.15"–0.20" |
| 8–10 pages (16–20 sheets) | ~0.25"–0.35" |
| 12+ pages (24+ sheets) | ~0.40"–0.50" |

Three booklets at 0.25" each = 0.75" added to the stack. **The current 6.5" box depth does NOT have room for this.** The stack was already snug at 6.5". Adding 0.50"–0.75" of booklet thickness means either:

- **(A) Increase box depth** from 6.5" to ~7.0"–7.25". Changes the briefcase proportions (front face ratio drops from 2.0:1 to ~1.85:1 — still acceptable). Affects all exterior dimensions and artboard sizes.
- **(B) Reduce tray wall heights** to reclaim the space. Tray 1 walls could drop from 0.75" to 0.50" (pen is only 0.6" — tight but possible). Tray 2 walls could drop from 0.875" to 0.625" (tissues at 0.75" would stick up slightly — may be acceptable). Tray 3 is already tight around the mug — hard to reduce.
- **(C) Keep booklets very thin** — 4–6 pages each, ~0.15"–0.20" thick. Three booklets = ~0.50" total. Easier to absorb. But limits content.
- **(D) Booklets rest inside tray walls, on top of items.** Instead of sitting on top of the tray rim, each booklet sits down inside the tray, resting on the items. This adds zero height to the stack — the booklet thickness is absorbed by the tray wall height. But it means items aren't in chalk-outline recesses (the booklet covers them), and the booklet edges sit below the tray rim, making it harder to grab.

**Recommendation:** Option A (slightly deeper box) is the cleanest. Option C (thin booklets) is the cheapest. Resolve this with sourcing before updating dimensional documents.

### What Changes About the Trays

**Tray width increases.** Removing the binder rings eliminates the 1.25" ring clearance from the back wall. All trays gain that width.

| Dimension | Old | New | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Usable tray width | 6.25" | 7.5" (full interior width) | +1.25" gained |
| Usable tray length | 12.5" | 12.5" (unchanged) | — |
| Tray Y-center offset | +0.625" (shifted toward front) | 0.0" (centered) | Trays are now centered in the case |

**Tray notches eliminated.** Trays no longer need back-edge notches or clearance for ring hardware. Simpler tray construction.

**Lift tabs relocate.** Tabs were on the back edge of each tray (nearest the rings, hidden under pages). With booklets sitting on top instead of pages threading through rings, the tabs can move to whichever edge is most accessible — likely the **front edge** (nearest the person) for easier grabbing. Or they could remain on the back edge if the booklet covers them until it's removed. Design decision TBD.

### What Changes About the Inside Lid

The booking frame, pockets, and check-in card are **UNCHANGED.** The lid no longer needs to act as a backstop for flipped pages. The lid angle is no longer critical — it can open to any comfortable angle. The 100-105° spec was driven by the page-catching requirement and can be relaxed.

### What Changes About the Back Wall

The interior back wall (-Y) is now a **blank interior surface.** No ring hardware, no mounting brackets, no spine. This surface could:
- Stay blank (simplest)
- Have a decorative binder ring graphic printed on it (nod to the concept)
- Have printed copy or OG character content
- Have a small decorative element

Design decision TBD. Not blocking any other work.

### What's Removed Entirely

- D-ring hardware (3 rings, mounting spine, mounting brackets)
- Ring diameter, ring spacing, and ring orientation specs
- Ring clearance zone (1.25" from back wall)
- Tray back-edge notches for ring clearance
- Page hole-punch specs (pages are now bound in booklets)
- Polaroid clear sheet protector sleeve in rings ("EVIDENCE: DO NOT REMOVE") — this element needs a new home. Options: tucked inside Booklet 1, tucked inside a lid pocket, or loose on top of Tray 1 under Booklet 1.
- Lid backstop angle requirement (100-105°)
- Page-flipping interaction mechanics
