## Narrative Framework (OG vs. Corporate Nemesis)

### The OG

The original creator of the Office Survival Kit. A veteran office survivor who built the kit as an underground, grassroots guide for fellow inmates of the corporate system. He wrote a 153-page manual -- the source material for everything in the kit.

**Voice:** Dry, deadpan, one-beat-per-line pacing. Never breaks character. Short and cryptic. Handwritten feel.

**Where he appears:**
- Sticky notes printed on item boxes
- Activity guide (his hidden playbook)
- Smuggled manual excerpts
- Hidden surfaces throughout the kit

**Sample voice:**
- "You're not special. Everyone removes it. Good instincts, though. You'll need those."
- "I'm not management. I'm not HR. I'm what happens when you stop believing promotions are real."

---

### Brad Sterling (B.S.) — The Corporate Nemesis

**Brad Sterling** — Chief Vision Officer & Founder, **Elevate Co.** Workplace Solutions Division. A hustle-culture entrepreneur who found the OG's kit, mass-produced it under the Elevate Co. brand, and is now selling it on Amazon. He genuinely thinks he "elevated" the OG's crude kit into a "premium consumer experience." His initials spell B.S. — OG knows, Brad doesn't.

- **Name:** Brad Sterling (B.S.) — LOCKED
- **Brand:** Elevate Co. — Workplace Solutions Division — LOCKED
- **Website:** bradsterling.com (vanity domain — named after himself, not his company)

**Voice:** Two modes — see `brad-copy-voice-guide.md` for full writing guide:
1. **Product Copy Brad** — Competent, dry, institutional. Genuinely good office humor in straight-faced formats. This is why Elevate Co. exists.
2. **Personal Brand Brad** — Hustle-culture, zero self-awareness, premium-stacking, credential-dropping. This is why people laugh at Brad.

**Where he appears:**
- Outer Amazon box — 100% Brad (complete copy in `outer-box-copy-v1.md`)
- Briefcase exterior — Brad's design + sparse OG bleed-through (see Exterior Copy Doc v4)
- Individual item box packaging — Brad's rebranded names and descriptions
- Interior branded inserts — welcome letter, About the Founder panel (on outer box inside lid)
- Belly band — Brad's corporate wrapper (drafted in Exterior Copy Doc v4)

**He is NOT self-aware.** The gap between his polished marketing and reality IS the joke.

**Comedy rule:** "Format stays buttoned-up, content gets unleashed." Every line earns its space. See `brad-sterling-profile.md` for the full comedy gradient and writing patterns.

**Sample voice (Product Copy Brad):**
- "The Executive Tactical Stylus™ — Precision-engineered writing instrument for the modern professional. Weighted barrel. Commanding ink flow. Makes a statement before you write a word."

**Sample voice (Personal Brand Brad):**
- "What it lacked was branding, individual packaging, trademark symbols, and someone willing to take full credit."

### The Tension

Every piece of packaging has two layers: what the nemesis wants you to see, and what the OG snuck in.

OG sticky notes are printed throughout as handwritten corrections and commentary on the nemesis's marketing. The recipient gradually discovers the OG's presence -- first through cryptic sticky notes, then through the defaced product guide (if Direction A is chosen), and finally through the hidden activity guide.

## Tone & Voice Notes

> **Full voice references live in dedicated character docs. This section is a quick summary — for writing, always consult the source docs.**

### The OG's Voice
- Sarcastic, dry, absurdist
- The office IS a prison — not a metaphor, just reality (see `world-rules.md`)
- Short, punchy, one-beat-per-line
- Never breaks character
- Weary amusement toward Brad, not rage — eye-roll energy
- Words/phrases: yard, cell, block, inmate, issued, contraband, "In the yard..."

### Brad Sterling's Voice
- **Two modes** — see `brad-copy-voice-guide.md` for full writing guide:
  1. **Product Copy Brad** — Dry, institutional, genuinely competent office humor. Format is the straight man, content escalates.
  2. **Personal Brand Brad** — Hustle-culture, zero self-awareness, premium-stacking, credential-dropping.
- The BETTER his product copy is, the funnier his personal brand becomes by contrast
- Trademark symbols on everything (Brad's nervous tic)
- Words/phrases: "engineered," "premium," "curated," "experience," "innovation," ™ everywhere

### The Comedy Rule — LOCKED
> **"Format stays buttoned-up. Content gets unleashed."**

The old 80/20 rule is dead. Official labels, FDA formatting, corporate letterhead stay polished and straight-faced. The words inside go for laughs on every line. Density is a feature — the reader who scans should laugh, the reader who reads closely should laugh harder. See `brad-sterling-profile.md` for the full comedy gradient.

### The Tension Between Them
- Brad's polished copy is the straight man
- The OG's sticky notes are the punchline
- The gap between marketing and reality IS the joke
- Brad is never in on the joke — he's earnest
- Three-layer architecture: Outer box (100% Brad) → Briefcase exterior (Brad + sparse OG) → Interior (OG's design + Brad's repackaging)

### Recurring Characters
See Exterior Copy Doc v4 for the Character Registry. Key characters: Kevin (the coworker), Gary (the inspector), Diane (HR). Kevin target density: 4-6 across full kit (4 exterior already placed). Continue in interior where the joke earns it — don't force it.

### Words/phrases to AVOID
- Anything that sounds like actual workplace advice
- Puns (too cute — Brad's product copy is dry, not clever)
- Exclamation points (keep it dry)
- Winking at the reader (the straight face IS the technique)
- Breaking the world rules — nobody acknowledges the office/prison comparison as a comparison

## Hidden Copy -- Easter Eggs

**Tray walls:** The inside walls of trays (visible only after items are removed) are copy surfaces for hidden messages. These are easter eggs -- discovered by the curious. OG character voice, short, rewarding. Zero additional COGS since trays are already being printed.

**Tray undersides:** Visible when trays are lifted. Additional hidden copy surface for OG messages or narrative breadcrumbs.

**Inside the mug:** A small printed card or tag placed inside the mug. "You looked inside. Smart. That instinct will keep you alive in here." The final easter egg in the entire kit.

**Booking frame back:** Comedy text, institutional fine print, or booking instructions on the reverse side of the photo prop.
