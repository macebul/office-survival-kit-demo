## Kit Descriptions ✅ APPROVED

### SHORT — Packaging (recipient reads)
> Welcome to your sentence. Inside: everything you need to survive the cubicle, mark your territory, and make it to Friday. Standard issue. No parole.

### SHORT — Selling/Amazon (buyer reads)
> For the coworker who gets it. For the friend serving their time. For anyone who's earned their ink. This is their kit.

### LONG — Amazon listing (buyer reads)
> Somewhere right now, someone is sitting in a meeting that could've been an email. Someone is watching their lunch get "borrowed" from the fridge. Someone is one "per my last email" away from snapping. This kit is for them. Inside: protection, contraband, ink they've earned, territory markers, and cleanup supplies for whoever loses the next meeting. Or maybe it's for you. Either way — you've earned it.

## Tone & Voice Notes

**This copy should feel like:**
- Sarcastic, dry, absurdist
- Prison metaphor played completely straight
- Bureaucratic language used for dark humor
- Corporate jargon twisted into threats

**Words/phrases TO use:**
- Yard, cell, block, inmate, issued, contraband
- HR, meeting, break room, cubicle
- Deniable, untraceable, confiscated
- "In the yard..." (recurring setup)

**Words/phrases to AVOID:**
- Anything that sounds like actual workplace advice
- Puns that break the prison voice
- Exclamation points (keep it dry)
